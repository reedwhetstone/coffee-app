## Thesis

AI is excellent at removing incidental friction: formatting, lookup, scaffolding, repetitive cleanup, and other forms of mechanical drag. It becomes dangerous in learning contexts when it removes load-bearing friction: retrieval, articulation, revision, and the uncomfortable effort that actually builds judgment.

## Counterintuitive Angle

The problem is not that AI is "too smart" for students. The problem is that it is too good at making unfinished thinking look finished. That means the real design challenge is not capability. It is whether the product preserves the parts of difficulty that learning depends on.

### 2. Learning depends on effort that often feels inefficient

- Use the Nature review to ground spacing and retrieval practice.
- Make the key point: the feeling of ease is a bad proxy for durable learning.
- Translate that into plain language: some struggle is the mechanism, not the obstacle.

### 3. AI removes both kinds of friction, not just the useless kind

- Introduce the MIT Media Lab / arXiv cognitive debt preprint.
- Keep caveats explicit: preprint, small sample, narrow essay-writing task.
- Add Anthropic's education report to show the behavioral pattern at scale: students often use Claude for creating/analyzing, and many conversations are direct-answer oriented.
- Use Gerlich as correlational support for the cognitive offloading frame, with explicit limits.

### 4. Why the calculator analogy breaks

- Calculators compress a narrow operation after foundations are built.
- LLMs can collapse the whole loop: framing, drafting, synthesis, and revision.
- The distinction is not "tool vs no tool" but augmentation after mastery vs substitution before mastery.

### 5. The real risk is incentives, not possibility

- People can still think deeply with AI. I do.
- The problem is the default path gets easier, faster, and good-enough.
- This creates a bifurcation: AI compounds strong thinkers and substitutes for developing ones.

### 6. What I would protect

- Product and education design prescription.
- Let AI handle incidental friction.
- Protect retrieval, first-pass articulation, reflection, and error correction.
- Point to Anthropic's Learning Mode as evidence that product design can push toward understanding instead of answer delivery.

### 7. Closing

- Ease sells itself.
- Load-bearing friction has to be defended on purpose.
- End with a memorable line about choosing the right friction before we forget why it mattered.

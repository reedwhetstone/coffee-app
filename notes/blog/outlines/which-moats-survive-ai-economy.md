# Outline: What If Your Moat Becomes a Prison?

**Pillar:** ai-first-product
**Tags:** ai, moats, strategy, agility
**Target word count:** 900–1,100
**Status:** drafted (PR #301)

## Thesis

AI does not merely change which moats survive. It changes what a moat is for. When software can be built and rebuilt quickly, durable assets matter most as stored energy for the next position. The long-term winner is not necessarily the company with the deepest moat, but the company capable of using its current advantages to supersede its current product.

Kodak is the warning. It had the technology, distribution, brand, capital, and expertise to lead digital photography. Its moats became organizational constraints because its incentives and identity were attached to film economics. A moat can become a prison.

## Structure

### 1. AI makes building cheap, but not position

- Engineering-time scarcity is weakening.
- Distribution, trust, operational history, regulation, and physical infrastructure still take elapsed time.
- The familiar question, "which moats survive?", is incomplete.

### 2. A moat should buy options

- Reframe moats as stored energy rather than permanent walls.
- Distribution launches the next product.
- Proprietary data reveals adjacent workflows.
- Regulatory access creates a protected window for reinvention.
- AI raises the option value of these assets because adjacent products are cheaper to test and build.

### 3. Kodak had the tools to win

- Kodak invented the first self-contained digital camera in 1975.
- It also possessed the brand, distribution, manufacturing knowledge, intellectual property, and cash to lead the transition.
- The failure was organizational, not technological. Film margins, incentives, and identity prevented action against the incumbent business.
- Core line: **A moat can become a prison.**
- Generalize the risk to deeply embedded workflows: a defensible position can protect an asset after the value chain moves elsewhere.

### 4. Agility means invalidating yourself

- Speed is how quickly a company moves; agility is whether it can change direction.
- AI can accelerate entrenchment when a company ships faster along the wrong trajectory.
- Real agility lives in incentives and decision rights: moving capital, people, and attention away from a successful product before decline is obvious.
- Cannibalization should be treated as a transfer of advantage, not sabotage.

### 5. The Purveyors test

- The website and scraper code are reproducible.
- The normalized dataset, shared schema, and operational history are more durable.
- Those assets still are not sacred. Their purpose is better coffee decisions, not preservation of the current interface.
- Closing question: **Does this advantage help preserve the current position, or help reach the next one?**
- Final claim: build moats, but do not confuse the moat with the destination.
